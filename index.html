The problem

Fitness apps assume the hard part is fitness.

They track pace. They push you harder. They don't know that for some people, the hardest part isn't the workout — it's what their body does during it. The racing heart. The breathlessness. The feeling that something is wrong.

"I used to run marathons. Then I had my first panic attack during a run. I haven't run since. That was three years ago."

— Anonymous user

The approach

Graded exposure — the same principle therapists use.

Tiny, controlled increases in exertion. Walk 5 minutes. Jog 15 seconds. Walk 3 minutes. Repeat. Your body learns — through experience, not logic — that the sensation is temporary and safe.

"I just want to go at all. Every other app wants me to go faster."

— Anonymous user

Questions

Is this therapy? +
No. SteadyPace is a wellness tool — not a medical device, therapeutic intervention, or substitute for professional treatment. It uses principles inspired by graded exposure but is not a clinical implementation. Always consult a healthcare provider if you have a diagnosed condition.
What if I panic during a session? +
Tap "I feel it starting." The app shifts to guided recovery with breathing cues, haptic feedback, and reassurance. It encourages you to slow down — not stop — helping your body learn the sensation passes.
How is this different from Couch to 5K? +
C25K assumes your challenge is fitness. SteadyPace assumes your challenge is anxiety. Plans adapt based on anxiety levels, not running speed. Success is measured by decreasing discomfort, not distance.
Do I need to be a runner? +
No. SteadyPace supports running, gym workouts, hiking, and general exercise. You tell us what you want to get back to and we build around that.
Can I cancel anytime? +
Yes. Cancel through your Apple ID settings. No questions, no guilt, no dark patterns.
